◇ How These Programs Were Ranked — Scoring Methodology ▾

Each program was scored across 6 weighted criteria matched to your professional identity: a systems-building, critical-care-comfortable, tech/data-inclined EMS physician drawn to austere, deployable, and global problems — with a preference for sun and outdoor living.

⚙

System Excellence & QI Culture

Legendary QA/QI programs, resuscitation performance, mature medical direction infrastructure — model agencies and data-driven cultures.

🚑

Operational Reps & Field Autonomy

Doc-in-the-car, dedicated response vehicles, command shifts, special operations exposure — real field responsibility.

🚁

Critical Care Transport & Aviation

Big air-med networks and robust CCT education — matches your high-acuity physiology interests and builds a rare skill stack.

🌍

Austere / Disaster / Global

Frontier medicine, SAR, wilderness, disaster planning, or global EMS development — the “deployable systems” dimension.

☀

Lifestyle Fit

Climate, outdoor access, day-to-day happiness. Deep winter and big-city grind that doesn’t match your preferences got penalized.

💲

Economic Survivability

Not the primary driver, but very high COL markets got nudged down unless training upside is truly unique and irreplaceable.
